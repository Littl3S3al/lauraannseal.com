about

Laura Ann Seal

I am a digital professional who has been working in design for almost a decade; with a background in fine art, architecture and then digital media I have a keen sense of design for both aesthetics and user experience.

I am very passionate about all things digital; my expertise includes diverse coding languages and even the creation of award winning digital games. When working on new projects I am always thinking, how do we exceed expectations and make something truly breathtaking?

ethos

The work that I do has a human centered approach which is considered in all things. I strive to make digital content which is in tune with the central drive of humanity: connection. My belief is that a website shouldnâ€™t be cold and clinical, but should feel like it was thoughtfully created by humans for humans.

I believe that as content creators, we have a responsibility to be detail orientated, building digital designs which are seamlessly constructed as well as honest in their communication.

Connection and accessibility are the fundamental aspects to my ethos and with them comes the opportunity to find creativity and spark enthusiasm in the audience. There is always an opportunity to find interactions which surprise, delight and inspire.

Design should also be robust, not trendy. I design things to last, the way our furniture used to. Although the web is always changing, our representation on the web should be steadfast and true.

Websites should make as many users as possible feel included. This why I also focus on low data usage and the capacity of the elderly, visually or audio impaired to navigate and understand the content in addition to other users.

This is the ethos I immerse myself in when I create websites. They may be something commonplace to most, overlooked and often accepted as a point of frustration; but the truth is that websites are the way that most people and businesses communicate their message to the world, and how it is designed and put together can really determine the success or failure of a venture.
